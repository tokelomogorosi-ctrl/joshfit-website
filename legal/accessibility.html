Accessibility Statement

Last updated 1 June 2026

Joshfit Personal Training wants the website to work for as many people as possible. This page describes how the site is built for accessibility, the limitations we know about, and how to flag a problem.

What we have done

  • Built to align with the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 at AA level
  • Text colour contrast against background tested at 4.5:1 or higher for body copy and 3:1 or higher for large display type
  • Every image has descriptive alternative text. Decorative images carry empty alt attributes
  • Every interactive element (links, buttons, form fields) is reachable by keyboard alone
  • Focus styles are visible and electric green on the dark background, easy to spot
  • Animations honour the user's prefers-reduced-motion setting. If you have animations turned off in your operating system, the site shows content immediately with no fade or movement
  • Video and audio players include native controls with mute, play, progress, and volume
  • The page works at standard browser zoom levels up to 200% without breaking layout
  • The site loads and reads even with JavaScript disabled (a no-JS fallback keeps content visible)

Known limitations

  • Video and audio content does not yet include captions or transcripts. We are adding these as a Phase 2 deliverable
  • The hero background uses a subtle 20-second breathing zoom. Users who have requested reduced motion will see a static hero instead
  • Some third-party widgets (Plyr media player) may lag behind the latest WCAG guidance in places

Enforcement procedure

Accessibility of websites is enforced under the Equality Act 2010. If you feel a complaint has not been handled fairly, you can contact the Equality and Human Rights Commission.

How this statement was prepared

The site was tested by Conversion Forge against the WCAG 2.2 checklist in June 2026 using a mix of automated tools and manual keyboard and screen-reader review.
